● Great question. These serve fundamentally different purposes in the architecture:                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Transformer Attention Heads (Cross-Chunk Processing)                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Purpose: Enable chunks to "see" each other and share context.                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Chunk 1: "Patient has ECOG PS 2"                                                                                                                                  
  Chunk 2: "with metastatic disease"                                                                                                                                
  Chunk 3: "Started on pembrolizumab"                                                                                                                               
  Chunk 4: "Prior chemotherapy failed"                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                    
  The transformer performs self-attention: each chunk attends to all other chunks.                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                    
  - Input: C chunk embeddings                                                                                                                                       
  - Output: C chunk embeddings (enriched with cross-chunk context)                                                                                                  
  - Operation: Pairwise (every chunk ↔ every other chunk)                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                    
  After transformer processing, Chunk 1 now "knows" about the metastases in Chunk 2 and the treatment in Chunk 3. The embedding for "ECOG PS 2" is now              
  contextualized by the rest of the document.                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Multiple heads can capture different relationship types:                                                                                                          
  - Head 1 might learn temporal/sequential dependencies                                                                                                             
  - Head 2 might learn semantic similarity between chunks                                                                                                           
  - Head 3 might learn negation scope ("no" in one chunk affecting interpretation of another)                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Gated MIL Confounder Queries (Aggregation)                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Purpose: Extract K specific confounder signals from the entire document.                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Query 1 (metastatic status): attends heavily to Chunk 2                                                                                                           
  Query 2 (performance status): attends heavily to Chunk 1                                                                                                          
  Query 3 (current treatment): attends heavily to Chunk 3                                                                                                           
  Query 4 (treatment history): attends heavily to Chunk 4                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                    
  The MIL queries perform cross-attention: K learnable query vectors attend to C chunks.                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                    
  - Input: C chunk embeddings + K learnable query vectors                                                                                                           
  - Output: K confounder representations                                                                                                                            
  - Operation: Many-to-few (C chunks → K confounders)                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Each query specializes to extract a different clinical factor. The gating mechanism (tanh × sigmoid) helps suppress irrelevant chunks for each query.             
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Key Differences                                                                                                                                                   
  ┌──────────────────┬───────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────┐                                                                          
  │      Aspect      │       Transformer Heads       │      MIL Confounder Queries       │                                                                          
  ├──────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤                                                                          
  │ Information flow │ Chunk ↔ Chunk                 │ Chunks → Queries (unidirectional) │                                                                          
  │                  │ (bidirectional)               │                                   │                                                                          
  ├──────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤                                                                          
  │ Query source     │ Data-derived (self-attention) │ Learned parameters                │                                                                          
  ├──────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤                                                                          
  │ Output dimension │ Preserves C chunks            │ Reduces to K confounders          │                                                                          
  ├──────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤                                                                          
  │ Purpose          │ Contextualization             │ Aggregation/extraction            │                                                                          
  ├──────────────────┼───────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤                                                                          
  │ What it learns   │ Inter-chunk relationships     │ What patterns to extract          │                                                                          
  └──────────────────┴───────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────┘                                                                          
  Analogy                                                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Transformer = A group discussion where everyone can hear everyone else. After the discussion, each person's understanding is enriched by what others said.        
                                                                                                                                                                    
  MIL Queries = K specialized interviewers, each with a specific question ("What's the performance status?" "Where are the metastases?"). Each interviewer listens  
  to the whole discussion but focuses on extracting their specific answer.                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                    
  In Code                                                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                    
  # After GRU encoding: C chunk embeddings                                                                                                                          
  chunk_embeddings = ...  # (C, dim)                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                    
  # Transformer: chunks talk to each other                                                                                                                          
  for layer in transformer_layers:                                                                                                                                  
      chunk_embeddings, _ = layer(chunk_embeddings)  # Still (C, dim), but enriched                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                    
  # MIL: K queries extract confounders                                                                                                                              
  confounders, attention = gated_mil_attention(chunk_embeddings)  # (K, dim)                                                                                        
  # attention[k, c] = how much query k attended to chunk c                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Why Both?                                                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Without transformer: Each MIL query sees chunks in isolation. Query for "metastatic status" can't leverage context from adjacent chunks.                          
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Without MIL: You'd need another aggregation mechanism. Mean pooling loses information. A single [CLS]-style token can't specialize for different confounders.     
                                                                                                                                                                    
  Together: Transformer enriches chunks with global context, then MIL queries extract K specialized confounder signals from the contextualized representation.
