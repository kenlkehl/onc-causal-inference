 A Theoretical Perspective: Why End-to-End Might Have Fundamental Limits                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                            
 The Information-Theoretic View                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                            
 Consider what information flows through your system:                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                            
 Text (high-dimensional, noisy)                                                                                                                             
     ↓ [Feature Extraction]                                                                                                                                 
 Representation (128-dim)                                                                                                                                   
     ↓ [Causal Head]                                                                                                                                        
 Y0, Y1, Propensity                                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                            
 The representation must compress text into 128 dimensions. What gets kept?                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                            
 - Propensity signal: Strong gradient, fully supervised → retained                                                                                          
 - Outcome signal: Strong gradient, fully supervised → retained                                                                                             
 - ITE signal: Weak gradient (indirect), partially supervised → may be discarded                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                            
 The model can achieve low loss by keeping propensity and marginal outcome signals while discarding the treatment effect heterogeneity signal. This is an   
 information bottleneck problem.                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                            
 The Identifiability View                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                            
 In causal inference, we distinguish:                                                                                                                       
 - Confounders: Affect both treatment and outcome (what you want)                                                                                           
 - Instruments: Affect treatment only                                                                                                                       
 - Prognostic factors: Affect outcome only                                                                                                                  
 - Mediators: Are affected by treatment                                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                            
 Your text contains ALL of these mixed together. The model has no way to know which features are confounders vs. prognostic factors vs. instruments. This   
 is the causal identification problem.                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                            
 LLM extraction works because you're telling the LLM "extract the CONFOUNDER" - you're providing causal identification externally.                          
                                                                                                                                                            
 The Optimization View                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                            
 Consider two local minima:                                                                                                                                 
 1. ITE-aware minimum: Y0(x) and Y1(x) are different functions, capturing treatment heterogeneity                                                           
 2. ITE-ignorant minimum: Y0(x) ≈ Y1(x) ≈ E[Y|X], ignoring treatment                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                            
 Both achieve similar factual outcome loss (since you only observe one). But (2) is "simpler" - fewer effective parameters. By Occam's razor (implicit in   
 neural network optimization), the model prefers (2).

 What you might be missing: Matching is primarily for estimating population-level effects. For patient-level ITE, you need to learn a function that maps    
 individual characteristics to individual effects - matching doesn't provide this.

 The Deeper Problem: Causal Inference Is Fundamentally Hard                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                            
 The reason end-to-end learning hits a ceiling is that causal inference requires learning counterfactual outcomes that are never observed. This is a        
 fundamental problem, not just an engineering challenge.                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                            
 What LLM Extraction Actually Does                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                            
 When you use LLM extraction, you're essentially doing:                                                                                                     
 1. Explicit variable selection: The LLM is told exactly what to extract (metastatic sites)                                                                 
 2. Noise removal: The extraction creates a clean, categorical representation                                                                               
 3. Inductive bias: The downstream model MUST use this variable because it's all it has                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                            
 This is similar to instrumental variable approaches in econometrics - you're using the LLM as a tool to isolate the causal signal from noise.              
                                                                                                                                                            
 Why This Might Be The Right Approach                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                            
 For real clinical text with unknown confounders, you might need:                                                                                           
 1. Domain expert input to identify what confounders to extract                                                                                             
 2. LLM (or structured extraction) to pull those confounders from text                                                                                      
 3. Causal ML methods (DragonNet, causal forest) on the extracted confounders                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                            
 This isn't a failure of deep learning - it's recognition that causal identification requires domain knowledge, not just pattern recognition.
