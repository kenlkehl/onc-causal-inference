 Brainstorm: Why Causal Forest Fails with 10 Confounders                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                            
 TL;DR                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                            
 Most likely cause: Sample size (N=1000) is insufficient for a DGP with 20 treatment×confounder interactions. This is a fundamental         
 statistical limitation, not an architecture problem.                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                            
 Critical diagnostic: Run pure oracle test (fit causal forest on true confounders, skip text). This will isolate whether the issue is       
 sample size or representation learning.                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                            
 Expected outcome: With N=1000 and 20 interaction terms, even oracle features will likely show poor ITE correlation. You'll need            
 N=5000-10000 for this DGP complexity.                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                            
 The Core Observation                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                            
 - 1 confounder + treatment interactions: Good ITE correlation                                                                              
 - 10 confounders + treatment interactions: Poor ITE correlation, even with:                                                                
   - Oracle test (patient_prompt with explicit confounders)                                                                                 
   - Numeric embeddings for quantitative values

 Understanding the DGP Complexity                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                            
 1-Confounder DGP                                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                            
 τ(X) ≈ sigmoid_diff(β₀ + β₁X₁ + γ₁T×X₁)                                                                                                    
 - 1-2 treatment interaction terms                                                                                                          
 - Simple linear-in-interactions surface                                                                                                    
 - Easy for forest to partition                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                            
 10-Confounder DGP (from metadata.json)                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                            
 τ(X) = σ(outcome_logit|T=1) - σ(outcome_logit|T=0)                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                            
 outcome_logit = intercept                                                                                                                  
     + 18 main effects (4 continuous + 14 categorical dummies)                                                                              
     + 2 confounder×confounder interactions                                                                                                 
     + treatment_coefficient × T                                                                                                            
     + Σ(20 treatment×confounder interactions × T)                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                            
 Critical insight: ITE is driven by 20 treatment×confounder interaction terms passed through a sigmoid difference. This is a highly         
 non-linear, 10-dimensional surface.                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                            
 Hypotheses for Poor Performance                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                            
 H1: Statistical Power / Sample Size                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                            
 The curse of dimensionality for heterogeneous treatment effects                                                                            
                                                                                                                                            
 - N=1000 patients, 5-fold CV → ~800 train per fold                                                                                         
 - Honest estimation halves effective sample for each split                                                                                 
 - min_samples_leaf=10 → leaves have ≤10% of samples                                                                                        
 - Effective samples per leaf: 800 × 0.5 × 0.1 ≈ 40 samples                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                            
 For 20 interaction terms, the "effective dimension" of τ(X) is high. You need samples across the full confounder space to estimate         
 heterogeneity.                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                            
 Rough calculation: With K confounders having C categories on average:                                                                      
 - Full interaction space: O(C^K) cells                                                                                                     
 - With 10 confounders, even coarse binning (C=3) gives 3^10 ≈ 59,000 cells                                                                 
 - N=1000 means most cells are empty or singleton                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                            
 Verdict: This is likely a major factor. Even with perfect feature extraction, 1000 samples may be fundamentally insufficient.              
                                                                                                                                            
 H2: Representation Bottleneck                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                            
 128-dimensional representation may not preserve interaction structure                                                                      
                                                                                                                                            
 The causal forest pipeline:                                                                                                                
 1. Neural network → 128-dim representation                                                                                                 
 2. Propensity + outcome heads (BCE loss)

 H3: Two-Stage Estimation Problem                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                            
 No end-to-end gradient flow for τ estimation                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                            
 Stage 1 optimizes:                                                                                                                         
 L = BCE(propensity) + BCE(outcome)                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                            
 This learns features that predict marginal propensity and outcome. But ITE depends on conditional differences in potential outcomes.       
                                                                                                                                            
 The key issue: Propensity/outcome prediction ≠ treatment effect heterogeneity. A feature might be highly predictive of outcome but have no 
  treatment interaction (no effect modification).

 # oracle_test.py                                                                                                                           
 import numpy as np                                                                                                                         
 import pandas as pd                                                                                                                        
 import json                                                                                                                                
 import re                                                                                                                                  
 from econml.dml import CausalForestDML                                                                                                     
 from sklearn.ensemble import RandomForestClassifier, RandomForestRegressor                                                                 
                                                                                                                                            
 # Load data                                                                                                                                
 df = pd.read_parquet("example_synthetic_data_ten_confounders/dataset.parquet")                                                             
 with open("example_synthetic_data_ten_confounders/metadata.json") as f:                                                                    
     metadata = json.load(f)                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                            
 confounders = metadata['confounders']                                                                                                      
 summary_stats = metadata['summary_statistics']                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                            
 def parse_patient_prompt(prompt_text, confounders):                                                                                        
     """Extract confounder values from structured patient_prompt."""                                                                        
     values = {}                                                                                                                            
     for conf in confounders:                                                                                                               
         name = conf['name']                                                                                                                
         # Pattern: "name: value" or "name = value"                                                                                         
         pattern = rf"{re.escape(name)}[:\s=]+([^\n,]+)"                                                                                    
         match = re.search(pattern, prompt_text, re.IGNORECASE)                                                                             
         if match:                                                                                                                          
             val = match.group(1).strip()                                                                                                   
             if conf['type'] == 'continuous':                                                                                               
                 try:                                                                                                                       
                     values[name] = float(val)                                                                                              
                 except:                                                                                                                    
                     values[name] = np.nan                                                                                                  
             else:                                                                                                                          
                 values[name] = val                                                                                                         
         else:                                                                                                                              
             values[name] = np.nan if conf['type'] == 'continuous' else 'unknown'                                                           
     return values                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                            
 # Build oracle features                                                                                                                    
 oracle_features = []                                                                                                                       
 for _, row in df.iterrows():                                                                                                               
     parsed = parse_patient_prompt(row['patient_prompt'], confounders)                                                                      
     oracle_features.append(parsed)                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                            
 oracle_df = pd.DataFrame(oracle_features)                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                            
 # Z-score continuous variables                                                                                                             
 for conf in confounders:                                                                                                                   
     if conf['type'] == 'continuous':                                                                                                       
         name = conf['name']                                                                                                                
         stats = summary_stats.get(name, {})                                                                                                
         mean = stats.get('mean', oracle_df[name].mean())                                                                                   
         std = stats.get('std', oracle_df[name].std())                                                                                      
         oracle_df[f'{name}_z'] = (oracle_df[name] - mean) / std                                                                            
                                                                                                                                            
 # One-hot encode categorical                                                                                                               
 for conf in confounders:                                                                                                                   
     if conf['type'] == 'categorical':                                                                                                      
         name = conf['name']                                                                                                                
         dummies = pd.get_dummies(oracle_df[name], prefix=name)                                                                             
         oracle_df = pd.concat([oracle_df, dummies], axis=1)                                                                                
                                                                                                                                            
 # Select features for causal forest                                                                                                        
 feature_cols = []                                                                                                                          
 for conf in confounders:                                                                                                                   
     if conf['type'] == 'continuous':                                                                                                       
         feature_cols.append(f"{conf['name']}_z")                                                                                           
     else:                                                                                                                                  
         for cat in conf['categories']:                                                                                                     
             col = f"{conf['name']}_{cat}"                                                                                                  
             if col in oracle_df.columns:                                                                                                   
                 feature_cols.append(col)                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                            
 X_oracle = oracle_df[feature_cols].values.astype(float)                                                                                    
 X_oracle = np.nan_to_num(X_oracle, nan=0.0)  # Handle any remaining NaN                                                                    
                                                                                                                                            
 T = df['treatment_indicator'].values                                                                                                       
 Y = df['outcome_indicator'].values                                                                                                         
 tau_true = df['true_ite_prob'].values                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                            
 # Fit causal forest                                                                                                                        
 cf = CausalForestDML(                                                                                                                      
     model_t=RandomForestClassifier(n_estimators=100, random_state=42),                                                                     
     model_y=RandomForestRegressor(n_estimators=100, random_state=42),                                                                      
     n_estimators=200,                                                                                                                      
     honest=True,                                                                                                                           
     inference=True,                                                                                                                        
     random_state=42,                                                                                                                       
     n_jobs=-1                                                                                                                              
 )                                                                                                                                          
 cf.fit(Y=Y, T=T, X=X_oracle)                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                            
 # Evaluate                                                                                                                                 
 tau_pred = cf.effect(X_oracle).flatten()                                                                                                   
 corr = np.corrcoef(tau_pred, tau_true)[0, 1]                                                                                               
 print(f"Oracle ITE Correlation: {corr:.4f}")                                                                                               
 print(f"Oracle ITE RMSE: {np.sqrt(np.mean((tau_pred - tau_true)**2)):.4f}")                                                                
 print(f"Predicted ITE range: [{tau_pred.min():.4f}, {tau_pred.max():.4f}]")                                                                
 print(f"True ITE range: [{tau_true.min():.4f}, {tau_true.max():.4f}]")
